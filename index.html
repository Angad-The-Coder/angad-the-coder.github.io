My WebDev Journey

During winter break of 2020, a couple of my friends and I were participating in a program called Technology Student Association (TSA). In TSA, there are many different events one may compete in, all targeting skills in various aspects of technology. As someone with a fascination for all things software, I had signed up with my friends for all of the software-related events. One of these events in particular was Webmaster. The goal of Webmaster is to create two websites: one advertising your school’s TSA charter, and the other—called your design brief—serving as response to a specific prompt. This year’s prompt for the design brief was to create a website about a fictional medical telecommunications company. Originally, as I had no experience in web development, my job was to help with all the graphic design needed to make our website attractive. However, since we had assigned all of the programming work to a single person, we needed someone else to help out and code our design brief website. As I had been interested in web development for a long time, I figured I might as well give it a shot and, over the span of winter break, I learned how to code in HTML/CSS/JS and completed my team’s design brief. As a result of my hard work, my team passed the regional competition and is set to participate in the statewide competition later this year. Ever since I made that website, I’ve been looking for new opportunities to explore the world of web development and refine my skills.
